Climate change is primarily driven by human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, industrial processes, agriculture, and land use changes. These activities release greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide and methane, trapping heat in the atmosphere and causing global warming, leading to detrimental impacts on ecosystems, weather patterns, and human societies.







Extinction of species

1 / 3
2 / 3
3 / 3


The causes of animal extinction are diverse and often interconnected. They include habitat loss due to deforestation, pollution, climate change, overhunting or poaching, introduction of invasive species, and human activities such as urbanization and infrastructure development. These factors disrupt ecosystems, decrease biodiversity, and directly threaten the survival of many species.

Pollution is caused by various human activities such as industrial emissions, vehicle exhaust, burning fossil fuels, agricultural practices, improper waste disposal, and deforestation. These activities release harmful substances such as greenhouse gases, particulate matter, toxic chemicals, and plastic waste into the air, water, and soil, leading to environmental degradation and health hazards.







Deforestation

1 / 3
2 / 3
3 / 3

Deforestation arises from human activities such as agriculture, logging, and urban expansion. Clearing forests for farmland, timber extraction, and development diminishes vital habitats and reduces the number of trees that absorb carbon dioxide, exacerbating climate change. Economic interests often drive deforestation, impacting biodiversity, water cycles, and indigenous communities.
